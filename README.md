# Customer Lifetime Value Prediction Using Linear Regression

## 1. Background and Overview

Customer acquisition and retention are among the most important drivers of long-term profitability in e-commerce. While acquiring new customers can be costly, retaining existing customers and increasing their value over time often provides a greater return on investment. Understanding which customer characteristics and behaviors are associated with higher Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) enables organizations to make more informed decisions regarding marketing spend, customer engagement, and retention strategies.

The objective of this project was to identify the factors most closely associated with customer lifetime value and evaluate how accurately LTV can be estimated using customer behavioral, engagement, and transactional data. By analyzing customer purchasing patterns and engagement metrics, the project seeks to provide actionable insights that help businesses prioritize resources toward high-value customer segments and improve long-term revenue generation.

---

## 2. Data Structure Overview

The dataset contains 6,000 customer records and includes information related to customer tenure, purchasing behavior, engagement levels, satisfaction, and loyalty program participation. Key variables include account age, average order value, total orders, browsing frequency, engagement scores, satisfaction ratings, and price sensitivity indicators. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) was calculated using average order value and total orders to estimate the total revenue generated by each customer.

To better understand the drivers of customer value, customers were analyzed across multiple dimensions including account age, loyalty membership status, browsing behavior, engagement levels, and purchasing activity. This structure provided a comprehensive view of both behavioral and transactional factors that may influence long-term customer value and retention.

---

## 3. Executive Summary

The analysis found that customer lifetime value is highly concentrated among a relatively small segment of customers. The distribution of LTV was heavily right-skewed, indicating that a small group of high-value customers generates a disproportionately large share of overall revenue. Additionally, customers with longer account tenure consistently demonstrated higher lifetime value, highlighting the importance of customer retention initiatives.

Two linear regression models were developed to evaluate the factors associated with customer value. The first model utilized customer behavioral and engagement metrics and explained approximately 39% of the variation in customer lifetime value. A second model incorporating transactional variables such as total orders and average order value significantly improved predictive performance, explaining approximately 81% of the variation in LTV. These results indicate that purchase behavior remains the strongest predictor of customer value, while engagement and loyalty metrics provide meaningful additional insight into long-term customer profitability.

---

## 4. KPI Summary

The average customer lifespan was approximately 31 months, suggesting a relatively stable customer base with moderate long-term retention. Correlation analysis identified total orders, average order value, account age, loyalty membership status, and engagement score as the variables most strongly associated with customer lifetime value. Loyalty program participants generated higher LTV on average than non-members, indicating that customer loyalty initiatives may contribute positively to revenue growth.

Analysis of the top 10% of customers by lifetime value revealed several distinguishing characteristics. High-value customers maintained significantly longer account tenures, placed substantially more orders, and generated higher average order values than the overall customer population. These customers also demonstrated higher engagement levels and lower price sensitivity, suggesting that highly engaged customers are more likely to remain active and generate greater long-term value for the business.

---

## 5. Recommendations

Based on the findings, the organization should prioritize strategies that increase customer retention and engagement. Since account age and engagement were both positively associated with lifetime value, initiatives focused on improving customer experience, encouraging repeat purchases, and strengthening customer relationships are likely to produce meaningful revenue gains over time. Special attention should be given to customers during the early stages of their lifecycle, where proactive engagement efforts may improve long-term retention outcomes.

The results also support continued investment in loyalty programs and personalized marketing initiatives. Loyalty members consistently demonstrated higher lifetime value, suggesting that incentives designed to encourage repeat purchasing behavior may provide a positive return on investment. Additionally, identifying customers with characteristics similar to existing high-value customers can help target marketing efforts more effectively, improve customer retention, and maximize long-term profitability.
